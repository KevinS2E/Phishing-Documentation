Common signals

What to review in a few seconds

The goal is not to memorize complex rules. It is to recognize patterns that often appear in fake or manipulative messages.

✍️

Writing mistakes

Fraud messages often include spelling mistakes, odd phrasing, or poor translations.

🌐

Odd domains

A sender with strange names, public email domains, or small variations can be an important clue.

⏱️

Artificial urgency

Phrases like "act now" or "your account will be blocked" try to make you respond without thinking.

Safe path

What to do when an email feels suspicious

You do not need to know whether it is 100 percent fraud to act correctly. The right move is to stop and report it.

1

Do not interact

Avoid opening links, downloading attachments, or replying to the sender until the message is verified.

2

Report in Outlook

Use the "Report Phishing" option so the security team receives and analyzes the email.

3

Delete after reporting

Delete the message only after reporting it and when you no longer need to keep it as evidence.
